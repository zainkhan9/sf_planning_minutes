11. Commission Comments/Questions

Commissioner Antonini:

- Requested that the Department schedule a briefing on affordable

housing in a joint session with the Redevelopment Agency.

Commissioner W. Lee:

- Re: Transportation Authority. Is the Planning Department and the Transportation Authority coordinating all transportation and land use projects? Secondly, Does the Transportation Authority use the sales tax funds for work on the Transportation Management Account, the Transportation Demand Management Program, and the Transit [Preferential] Streets Program. It is his understanding that the Transportation Authority has not provided, or will provide, any funding to the Planning Department next year. This is of great concern to him. He is requesting that the Executive Director of the Transportation Authority to come to the Planning Commission and explain to them what his plans are regarding this funding

- Re: Harvey Rose Audit and Supervisor McGoldrick's requirement to have an advisory board oversee the implementation of it. His proposal is to have one or two commissioners work with the Planning Department and provide, through the Commissioners, to the chair of the Land Use Committee the status and cost of implementing those proposals. Requested that this be calendared next week to appoint one or two commissioners to the committee.

- Re: Housing. Requested presentations from different City agencies to ensure the better coordination of what we are doing with other City agencies and to ensure that there is available funding next year--given our budget deficit. First, from the Landmarks Board he would like the Executive Director present to the Planning Commission an overview of their roles and responsibilities. Second, he requested a presentation from the Mayor's Office of Housing. Of the five major projects that are being reviewed now, where are they in the EIR process? But more importantly, are they paying the Planning Department for this work?

- He has requested that the Director of the Redevelopment Agency come before the Commission because they have cut out some projects; like the Mid-Market EIR for next year-at least I didn't see it in the budget that was presented to us. He wants them to tell us what their priorities are and what they are paying the Planning Department next year.

- He would also like the Mayor's Office of Economic Development come and tell us what their priorities are. What funding they are providing to the community and how that ties into the planning process and the Planning Department's bottom line.

- Finally, it would be appropriate to have the Muni Director here to present and educate us on their 5 or 10 year plan, and find out what funding sources they have for the planning staff to work on some of their projects.

Commissioner Hughes:

- Requested a copy of the Department of Building Inspection's Lawful Residential Demolition Requirements and a copy of the Major Exterior Alteration Ordinance.

Commissioner Feldstein:

- Will be happy to work with Supervisor McGoldrick's Land Use Committee. It is very important that this Commission develop a close working relationship with the Board.

- Thanked Friends of Planning who offered to send any of them to a training workshop on The Role of the Planning Commissioner offered by the University of California at Davis.
